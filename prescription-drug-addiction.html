Prescription Drug Addiction

The abuse of prescription drugs is increasing among all ages. People are prescribed these potent drugs to help combat medical, and or psychiatric conditions. We see users become addicted or dependent on the drug due to long term, or incorrect use. Adolescents and young adults are using the drug for its stimulating, euphoric, or sedating effects. These drugs are readily available to all ages, and commonly obtained from family members who are prescribed, peers, or doctors willing to prescribe any unnecessary medication. Prescription drugs can be classed into three categories including narcotics, depressants, and stimulants.

Narcotics

Narcotics such as OxyContin, Vicodin, and Percocet are analgesics or opioids. Patients get prescribed these drugs for moderate to severe physical pain, they act on the body's opioid receptors to produce morphine like effects.

Depressants

Deoressants such as Xanax, Valium, and Librium are classed as benzodiazepines. They act on our central nervous system to produce a calming effect. These medications are made for short term use, as they have a very high potential for addiction.

Stimulants

Stimulants such as Ritalin, Dexedrine, and Meridia are classed as a methylphenidate or dextroamphetamine. They are often prescribed to treat ADHD/ADD and other common ‘conditions’ such as asthma and narcolepsy. They are generally abused because of their energizing and euphoric effects on the body.
