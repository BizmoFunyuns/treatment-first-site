Alcohol Treatment

Alcohol addiction is a common illness displayed in many different forms. Most alcoholics develop the illness over years of drinking, with their need for the substance rising as their tolerance increases. Many users maintain themselves for years, keeping their lives intact, with relationships and occupations hanging by a thread. Eventually the need for alcohol takes control of their lives, with bodily function dropping to an unmanageable level. The effects of alcoholism are physical, emotional, and social. The illness takes over the addict's life, health, family, and occupations.

Common types of alcoholism:

  • Binge drinkers, who can go many days or weeks fighting a drink. Although once the urge takes over, they go on benders, resulting in the user drinking uncontrollably.
  • Social, after-work drinkers who find themselves consuming more and more alcohol over time to fulfill their growing tolerance.
  • All day consumers, drinking steadily throughout the day, waking up in the middle of the night for a drink.

The inability to control the consumption of alcohol is shown around our country and displayed in more than 3 million cases per year. Alcohol treatment begins with a specialized detoxification of the user, followed by a unique rehabilitation program to help ensure one's long term success in life long sobriety.
